A **Creative Commons (CC)** license is one of several public copyright licenses that enable the free distribution of an otherwise copyrighted works. It is used when an author wants to give people the right to share, use and build upon a works that they have created. CC provides an author flexibility (for example, they might choose to allow only non-commercial uses of their own work) and protects the people who use or redistribute an author's work, **so they donâ€™t have to worry about copyright infringement, as long as they abide by the conditions that are specified in the license by which the author distributes the work**.

There are several types of CC licenses. The licenses differ by several combinations that condition the terms of distribution.

To learn more about the CC and types of licenses that fall under free use, be sure to check: <https://creativecommons.org/legalmusicforvideos>

-   [Newgrounds Audio](http://www.newgrounds.com/audio/)
-   [Soundcloud](https://soundcloud.com/groups/creative-commons)
-   [ocremix](http://ocremix.org/)
-   [incompetech](http://incompetech.com/music/royalty-free/)

Remember, that you should read the copyright license and make sure it is a license that allows you to use their work freely or non commercially as long as credit is given.

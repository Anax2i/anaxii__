  
这些年来，[osu!](ZH:Glossary#osu! "wikilink") 社群已经发展出了独有的一套术语，所以新人可能会看得一头雾水。

本页面收集了此类词汇以供参考，多数取自[这一篇文](http://osu.ppy.sh/p/formatpost&id=21575)。欢迎大家进行增添和修改。

A
-

### Abandoned Wasteland

　一个隐藏的版块。如果光凭名字无法让你理解这是一个什么样的版，通常我们会这样和管理员们解释："Put stuff we don't really need in here.（把没什么存在价值的东西丢到这里。）"。当一篇文浪费了资源，就会被移到那里。

### AiMod

　一个[编辑器中的功能](ZH:Beatmap_Editor "wikilink")，可以透过同时按下 Ctrl+Shift+A 启用。Aimod 可以侦测出常见的可能造成谱面无法被 Rank 的原因，因此作图或是 [Mod](ZH:modding "wikilink") 图时应考虑使用此功能。

### Approval

　当一张[谱面由于某些原因](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink")（长度、分数等等）而无法被 Rank，但[谱面审核团队的成员们认可该谱面时](ZH:Beatmap_Appreciation_Team "wikilink")，就会被归类于 Approved 类。App 图有自己的线上排行榜，但这些谱面所提供的分数并不会被计入玩家的 Ranked Score（Ranked 分数）中。可以透过其独特的火球图示（一般 Ranked 图为心形图示）来分辨出这些谱面。

### Authentic

　这个词用来表示一张[谱面是完全](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink")（歌曲、设定、[物件安排等](ZH:Glossary#Object "wikilink")）仿制[ DS 游戏的](ZH:Glossary#DS_games "wikilink")[谱面](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink")。

B
-

### Bancho

　一个由 [peppy](ZH:peppy "wikilink") 所开发的易于扩充的服务器，代替了第三方 IRC 服务器来负责管理所有 [osu!](ZH:Glossary#osu! "wikilink") 的线上内容。玩家在开启 osu! 时会自动连上 Bancho。

### Banchobot

　一个由 Echo49 所开发的自动回复程式，以显示某些资讯和公告游戏相关讯息的方式在游戏聊天室中帮助玩家。

### BAT

　即 **[Beatmap Appreciation Team（谱面审核团队）](ZH:Beatmap_Appreciation_Team "wikilink")**的缩写，是一群主要负责将符合规定的[谱面](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink") [Rank](ZH:Ranked_beatmap "wikilink") 或是 [Approve](ZH:Approved_Beatmap "wikilink") 的玩家。需要注意的是目前osu!已经没有BAT一词的说法。

### Beat

　**节拍**，用在[编辑器中时间轴方面的名词](ZH:Editor "wikilink")。是指一首歌依据其 [BPM（每分钟节拍数）](ZH:Glossary#Timing "wikilink") 切割出来的一单位时间。在拥有正确[Timing](ZH:Glossary#Timing "wikilink") 的[谱面中](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink")，每一拍都会正确对应在歌曲的节奏上。在时间轴上，一个完整的节拍长为两条白线之间的长度。随着 [Snap Divisor](ZH:Beat_Snap_Divisor "wikilink") 设定的不同，一个节拍还可以被划分得更细：红线代表半拍，蓝线代表四分之一拍，黄线代表八分之一拍，而紫线代表三分之一拍和六分之一拍。

### Beat Spacing

　一种作图的原则，描述两个[打击圈圈或](ZH:Glossary#Hit_Circle "wikilink")[滑条之间的空间距离应呼应其于时间上的距离长度](ZH:Slider "wikilink")，例如节拍相近的两个圈圈应摆放得较靠近，反之亦然。物件距离的一致性通常是 [Rank](ZH:Ranked_beatmap "wikilink") 的条件之一，因为这能让玩家很快就明白可以用固定的速度移动鼠标，方能更直观地游玩。将物件摆得太近会误导玩家点得太快，而摆得太远则会强迫玩家得快速移动鼠标才能及时点到圈圈。
　Beat Spacing 原则的一个常见例外是[重叠的](ZH:Glossary#Stacked "wikilink")[打击圈圈](ZH:Glossary#Hit_Circle "wikilink")。要确保摆放距离一致，可以在[编辑器的右手边启用](ZH:Editor "wikilink") [Distance Snap（间距控制）功能](ZH:Distance_Snap "wikilink")。然后，按着 Alt 并拉动右上角的滑杆就可以调整至你想要的 Beat Spacing 数值。这个数值愈高，[谱面就会愈难](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink")。

### Beatmap

　**谱面**，也可以简称为 **map（图）**。在 osu! 中类似于游戏的关卡，像是[ DS 游戏中的那些](Glossary#DS_games "wikilink")。最基本的谱面定义包含了：一首歌，按照音乐节奏安排的[打击圈圈](ZH:Glossary#Hit_Circle "wikilink")、[滑条和](ZH:Slider "wikilink")（或）[大轮盘](ZHGlossary#Spinner "wikilink")，另外也可以有一张背景图片或影片，甚至[故事板](ZH:Storyboarding "wikilink")。谱面的制作者通常被称呼为 beatmapper 或简略为 mapper。谱面可以透过 [BSS（谱面发布系统）上传给任何人游玩](ZH:Glossary#BSS "wikilink")，并被 [Mod](ZH:Modding "wikilink")。若一张谱面被认为不错，它就会被 [Rank](ZH:Ranked_beatmap "wikilink") 或在某些情况下被 [Approve](ZH:Approved_Beatmap "wikilink")。

### Break

　**休息时间**，指一段在[谱面中没有](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink")[物件出现](ZH:Glossary#Object "wikilink")，而[血量条的](ZH:Glossary#Life_Bar "wikilink") [Drain](Glossary#Drain "wikilink") 也会暂时中止的段落，可以让玩家的手臂稍作冷却并调整一下自己的控制器。休息时间中会出现图示来提示玩家的表现是良好（血量条超过某一程度）还是不佳（血量条低于该程度）。在 [DS 游戏和有](ZH:Glossary#DS_games "wikilink")[故事板的谱面中](ZH:Storyboarding "wikilink")，玩家表现也会影响到谱面背景故事的发展。大致来说，休息时间应尽可能在每四十秒至八十秒的游玩时间插入一次，并且持续超过五至十五秒，但根据各首歌曲的编排不同，可以安排休息时间的时机也可能会有出入。

### BSS

　**Beatmap Submission System，[谱面发布系统](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink")**。一个可供使用者发布其谱面至 Beatmap Help（作图问题）版或是 Completed Beatmaps（已完成谱面）版给人观看、评论和 [Mod](ZH:Modding "wikilink") 与 [Rank](ZH:Ranked_beatmap "wikilink") 的系统。可视情况选择 [Standard Submission（一般发布）或](ZH:Submission "wikilink") [Full Submission（完整发布）](ZH:Submission "wikilink")。

### Bubble

　[谱面的其中一个](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink") [Modding](ZH:modding "wikilink") 阶段，当一位 [BAT](ZH:Beatmap_Appreciation_Team "wikilink")、[GMT](ZH:Global_Moderation_Team "wikilink") 对该谱面的任何方面都觉得不错时，就会将其 Bubble，提名为[ Rank 图](ZH:Ranked_beatmap "wikilink")。Bubbled 图在其讨论串的标题会多出一个泡泡的图案。如果再一位 [BAT](ZH:Beatmap_Appreciation_Team "wikilink") 或是 [GMT](ZH:Global_Moderation_Team "wikilink") 对此复议，则此谱面就会被 [ Rank](ZH:Ranked_beatmap "wikilink")。

### Build

　[osu!](ZH:Glossary#osu! "wikilink") 的**版本**。每个版本都包含了改进、新东西和前一个版本所含问题的修正。版本分为两种：[测试版本和](ZH:Glossary#Test_Build "wikilink")[公开版本](ZH:Glossary#Public_Build "wikilink")。利用[更新程序就可下载到最新版本](ZH:Glossary#Updater "wikilink")。

-

### Changelog

　**更新历程**，乃是 [peppy](ZH:peppy "wikilink") 条列每次 [osu!](ZH:Glossary#osu! "wikilink") 更新事项的页面。你可以在[这里](http://osu.ppy.sh/p/changelog)阅读。每天都会有新修改加入[测试版中](ZH:Glossary#Test_Build "wikilink")，这些修改最后会被加入[正式版](ZH:Glossary#Public_Build "wikilink")。

### Clap

　一种打击音效，属于 [Sample Set](ZH:Glossary#Sample_Set "wikilink") 的其中之一，通常以与 [Whistle](ZH:Glossary#Whistle "wikilink") 及 [Finish](ZH:Glossary#Finish "wikilink") 不同的方式于歌曲中用来强调某一部份或是告一段落。在[编辑器中默认的外貌和音效为拍掌](ZH:Editor "wikilink")。

### Combo

　Combo 这个词有两种意思：

-   一串连续的[打击圈圈和](ZH:Glossary#Hit_Circle "wikilink")[滑条](ZH:Slider "wikilink")，它们有同样的颜色，对应于[谱面背景歌曲的一部份](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink")。如果打中在每串 Combo 中最后的物件，并且评价为[喝或](ZH:Score#Katu "wikilink")[激](ZH:Score#Geki "wikilink")，玩家就会获得[血量](ZH:Glossary#Life_Bar "wikilink")。每个[大轮盘则是独立的](ZH:Glossary#Spinner "wikilink") Combo。一串 Combo 中的[物件数应维持于约](ZH:Glossary#Object "wikilink") 20 个以下，并低于该难度可接受的最高 Combo 长度。

<!-- -->

-   **连击数**，即玩家在没有 [Miss](ZH:Glossary#Miss "wikilink") 的情况下连续击中的物件数量。游戏中会在左下角显示。每当玩家成功击中[打击圈圈](ZH:Glossary#Hit_Circle "wikilink")、[滑条的头尾与小圈圈](ZH:Silder "wikilink")、[大轮盘就会增加连击数](ZH:Glossary#Spinner "wikilink")。当连击数达到一定数量时，画面上就会出现星星喷泉。只要一个 Miss 就会让连击数归零。在玩家击中物件时，除了[滑条中的小圈圈只提供](ZH:Slider "wikilink") 10 分，以及[大轮盘量表中的每一个阶段也都只会提供](ZH:Glossary#Spinner_Metre "wikilink") 100 分，所获得的分数都会以连击数进行加乘。毫无 [Miss](ZH:Glossary#Miss "wikilink") 地完成[谱面称之为](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink")[完全连击](ZH:Glossary#Full_Combo "wikilink")，或简称为 FC 了一首歌，这是获得 S 以上[评价的必备条件](ZH:Glossary#Grade "wikilink")，达成时会在结果画面显示 **Perfect!** 字样。

### Combo Fire

　**连击火焰**，在[应援团中被称为激励之火](ZH:Glossary#Ouendan "wikilink")（The Flames of Encouragement）。当玩家达成 30 以上的[连击数时](ZH:Glossary#Combo "wikilink")，萤幕底部便会燃起火焰，象征其熊熊燃烧的灵魂。当连击数达到 60、100、150、200、250，及最后的 300 时火焰便会升高。在设定中可以调整连击火焰的最高高度。它可以被停用。

  
**注意：**这个功能目前已从游戏中被移除。

D
-

### Deathstream

　**死亡连打**，通常是指一串包含了节奏快速的连续[物件和](ZH:Glossary#Object "wikilink")（或）摆放方式复杂的[连打](ZH:Stream "wikilink")。多出现于高难度的[谱面中](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink")。比如著名的THE BIG BLACK。

### Difficulty stars

　**困难度星数**，或简称**星数**，用来表达一张[谱面的困难度](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink")。依照公式计算，总共会有 0 至 10 颗星。你可以透过在[编辑器进行测试模式](ZH:Editor "wikilink")，或是于歌曲选单上来确认一张[谱面的星数](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink")。

### Difficulty versions

　**难度**，一张[谱面通常要有二至四个不同阶层的难度](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink")，方能让新手与老手都能享受其乐趣。[谱面中的](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink")[物件数](ZH:Glossary#Object "wikilink")、[物件是否依照主](ZH:Glossary#Object "wikilink")、副节奏安排，或是 [Combo](ZH:Glossary#Combo "wikilink") 的长度、[Beat Snapping](ZH:Glossary#Beat_Spacing "wikilink") 的数值，以及[谱面的难度设定如](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink") [Drain 速度](ZH:Glossary#Drain "wikilink")、整体难度（OD）、[打击圈圈的大小等等](ZH:Glossary#Hit_Circle "wikilink")，这些都会影响到一张[谱面的难度](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink")。[星数是用来表示一张](ZH:Glossary#Difficulty_stars "wikilink")[谱面的难度的方法之一](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink")。[osu!](ZH:Glossary#osu! "wikilink") 网页将难度划分为四种标准：Easy（简单）、Normal（普通）、Hard（困难）、Insane（疯狂）和 Extreme（极限），不过作图者仍可以为其[谱面的难度取一个自己喜欢的名字](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink")。

### Drain

　指在游玩[谱面时](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink")，除了[休息时间以外的过程中](ZH:Glossary#Break "wikilink")[生命条降低的现象](ZH:Glossary#Life_Bar "wikilink")。作图者可以在[编辑器中的](ZH:Editor "wikilink") [Song Setup](ZH:Song_Setup "wikilink") 视窗的 Difficulty 标签中调整其[谱面的](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink") Drain 速度。Drain 愈高，该[谱面就会愈难](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink")。Drain 是 [Combo](ZH:Combos "wikilink") 不应太长的主要原因，否则玩家将无法频繁补回血量。

### DS games

　**DS 游戏**，是[押忍！战斗！应援团](http://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E6%8A%BC%E5%BF%8D%EF%BC%81%E6%88%B0%E9%AC%A5%EF%BC%81%E6%87%89%E6%8F%B4%E5%9C%98)（又称**应援团**或是**应援团一**）、[燃烧！热血韵律魂 押忍！战斗！应援团 2](http://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E7%87%83%E7%87%92%EF%BC%81%E7%86%B1%E8%A1%80%E9%9F%BB%E5%BE%8B%E9%AD%82_%E6%8A%BC%E5%BF%8D%EF%BC%81%E6%88%B0%E9%AC%A5%EF%BC%81%E6%87%89%E6%8F%B4%E5%9C%982)（多数时候都直接称为**应援团二**）和[精英节拍特工](http://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E7%B2%BE%E8%8B%B1%E8%8A%82%E6%8B%8D%E7%89%B9%E5%B7%A5)（**Elite Beat Agents**，简称 **EBA**）等游戏的通称。它们都是由 iNIS 为任天堂 DS 掌上型游乐器开发的节奏游戏，在游玩和外观上都有许多相似之处。[osu!](ZH:Glossary#osu! "wikilink") 的原型就是这些游戏。

E
-

### EBA

-   **Elite Beat Agents**，中译**精英节拍特工**，是 [osu!](Glossary#osu! "wikilink") 参考的 [DS 游戏之一](Glossary#DS_games "wikilink")。
-   该游戏中可玩角色的通称，例如 The EBA 或是 The Agents 。

### Etna Slider

　**Etna 滑条**，短、直的滑条，通常只有半[拍子长](ZH:Glossary#Beat "wikilink")，一拍中会出现两个。典型的 Etna 滑条通常一次由 6 至 8 个滑条连打组成。尽管 Etna 滑条不包含[折返箭头](ZH:Repeats "wikilink")，一串连打中的最后一个滑条是可以有[折返箭头的](ZH:Repeats "wikilink")。此用词来自[这张谱面](http://osu.ppy.sh/forum/viewtopic.php?f=14&t=655)，其中该类滑条于背景影片出现魔界战记的人物 [Etna](http://disgaea.wikia.com/wiki/Etna) 时出现。这个用词造就了[术语页面的诞生](ZH:Glossary "wikilink")。

### Extended Chat

　**扩张聊天面板**，透过在 [osu!](ZH:Glossary#osu! "wikilink") 中按下 F9 开启，此时聊天框就会扩张出用户列表，可利用数种条件来进行排序。其中只会显示出正在 [osu!](ZH:Glossary#osu! "wikilink") 线上的用户，即透过 [IRC](ZH:IRC "wikilink") 连接的用户不会出现。当游标移动到用户面板上时，就会显示出该用户的详细资讯。用户面板的颜色代表了用户当前的状态，参考下表：

-   深蓝色：闲置。
-   灰色：正在玩一张[谱面](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink")。如果你也有那张[谱面](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink")，你可以在对方的用户面板上点击，即可[观察对方](ZH:Glossary#Spectate "wikilink")。
-   浅蓝色：正在[观察其他玩家](ZH:Glossary#Spectate "wikilink")，或是在观看重播。
-   红色：正在编辑其自制的[谱面](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink")。
-   绿色：正在 [Mod](ZH:Modding "wikilink") 一张[谱面](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink")，意即正透过[编辑器观看](ZH:Editor "wikilink")。
-   紫色：正于[编辑器中测试一张](ZH:Editor "wikilink")[谱面](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink")。
-   棕色：正在[多人游戏大厅](ZH:multiplayer "wikilink")，或一间[多人游戏房间中](ZH:multiplayer "wikilink")。
-   金色：正在进行一场[多人游戏](ZH:multiplayer "wikilink")。
-   黑色：暂离（超过两分钟半没有动作）。
-   浅绿色：正在利用[谱面发布系统发布](ZH:Glossary#BSS "wikilink")[谱面](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink")。

F
-

### Finish

　一种打击音效，属于 [Sample Set](ZH:Glossary#Sample_Set "wikilink") 的其中之一，通常于歌曲中用来强调某一部份或是告一段落，比起[哨音听起来更加有力](ZH:Glossary#Whistle "wikilink")。在[编辑器中默认的外貌和音效为敲钹](ZH:Editor "wikilink")。

### Full Combo

　**完全连击**，简称为 **FC** 。完成一张[谱面的过程中没有任何](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink") [Miss](ZH:Glossary#Miss "wikilink") 的话，就会获得最大分数。

### Full Submission

　**完整发布**，是一种透过[谱面发布系统发布](ZH:Glossary#BSS "wikilink")[谱面](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink")，使其公开于 [osu!](ZH:Glossary#osu! "wikilink") 社群的方法。当[作图者新增或删除了背景图片](ZH:mapper "wikilink")、影片或 [Sample Set](ZH:Glossary#Sample_Set "wikilink") 的时候，就应选择完整发布。若为其他情况，则推荐[标准发布](ZH:Submission "wikilink")。

G
-

### GD

　即 **Guest Difficulty**，由上传[谱面者以外的人所制作的难度](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink")。

### Grade

　**评价**，又可称之为 **Rank**，但在下列情况中则多用前者，因为后者可能会与该词的另一个意涵混淆。即一个玩家在完成[谱面后所得到的评价](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink")。它会在结果发表画面和所有会显示分数的地方同总分一同显示。评价由低至高依序为：D、C、B、A、S 和 SS。要获得 S 评价，玩家必须在游玩[谱面的过程中没有任何](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink") Miss，并且 [100](ZH:Score#100 "wikilink") 数量最多只能是 [300](ZH:Score#300 "wikilink") 的十分之一。要获得 SS 评价，则玩家的打击必须全部都是 [300](ZH:Score#300 "wikilink")。另外还有 SH 和 SSH 两种评价（俗称为 **银 S** 和 **银 SS**。），它们分别代表开启了 [Hidden](ZH:Hidden "wikilink") 和 [Flashlight](ZH:Flashlight "wikilink") 两种 [Mod](ZH:Game_Modifiers "wikilink") 其中至少一者而获得的 S 和 SS 评价。

### Grid Snapping

　**网格校准**，一个在[ZH:Editor画面右边的功能按钮](ZH:Editor "wikilink")，可以强制让[作图者所放置的所有](ZH:Mapper "wikilink")[物件都校准到网线的交点上](ZH:Glossary#Object "wikilink")，并且也可以依时间差距长短固定两个[物件之间的距离](ZH:Glossary#Object "wikilink")。网格校准可以帮助物件彼此对齐，并让[作图者更容易以特定形状来进行安排](ZH:Mapper "wikilink")。

### Graveyard

　**坟场**，即论坛中的[Beatmap Graveyard（谱面坟场）](http://osu.ppy.sh/forum/viewforum.php?f=19)。超过一个月没有更新的[谱面就会被移至此版块](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink")，以维持其他[谱面相关版块整洁](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink")。如果想要「复活」自己的[谱面](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink")，只需到个人的用户页面，往下拉并展开 Beatmap 区块，在欲复活的[谱面名称右边点击](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink") Resurrect 即可。

### Hit Circle

  
*另可参考：[打击物件](ZH:Hit_Objects "wikilink")。*

　**打击圈圈**，在 [DS 游戏中则称为](ZH:Glossary#DS_games "wikilink") Hit Marker（打击目标）。是三种[物件](ZH:Glossary#Object "wikilink")（其他两种为[滑条和](ZH:Slider "wikilink")[大轮盘](ZH:Glossary#Spinner "wikilink")）之中玩家最可能常常碰到的[物件](ZH:Glossary#Object "wikilink")。其外观为一个有色实心圆圈，圆心中央有数字（确切数字依其于一串 [Combo](ZH:Glossary#Combo "wikilink") 中的排序而定）和另一个在其周围逐渐收缩的空心圆圈（即[缩小圈圈](ZH:Glossary#Approach_Circle "wikilink")）。当[缩小圈圈一碰到打击圈圈的瞬间](ZH:Glossary#Approach_Circle "wikilink")，玩家应迅速进行[打击](ZH:Glossary#Tap "wikilink")，即可获得分数（50、100、300），分数大小由这次[打击的准确率而定](ZH:Glossary#Tap "wikilink")。如果该打击圈圈是一串 [Combo](ZH:Glossary#Combo "wikilink") 的结尾，则可能会获得 50、[喝和](ZH:Score#Katu "wikilink")[激三种评价的其中之一](ZH:Score#Elite_Beat! "wikilink")。对打击圈圈进行[打击时会微微补充](ZH:Glossary#Tap "wikilink")[血量条](ZH:Glossary#Life_Bar "wikilink")。

### Hold Slider

　**按压滑条**，一种会[折返多次的](ZH:Repeats "wikilink")[滑条](ZH:Slider "wikilink")，通常玩家可以不需跟随其路径，只需将游标移至其上并按住即可完成[打击](ZH:Glossary#Tap "wikilink")。按压滑条通常用于歌曲中的连续性节奏，也可以用[堆叠的](ZH:Glossary#Stacked "wikilink")[打击圈来代替](ZH:Glossary#Hit_Circle "wikilink")。

### Jump

　　由 **Kudos** 和 **[osu!](ZH:Glossary#osu! "wikilink")** 二词混合而来。指用来奖励时常 [Mod](ZH:mod "wikilink") 的一套评级系统。[作图者可以赠送一位向其提供建设性](ZH:mapper "wikilink") [Modding](ZH:mod "wikilink") 文的用户 Kudosu。攒积起来的 Kudosu 可以提升一张[谱面的优先度](ZH:Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink")。更多关于 Kudosu 系统的资讯可参考[这里](http://osu.ppy.sh/forum/viewtopic.php?f=9&t=10245)。

L
-

### Lead-in

A time period of silence, up to three seconds long, which can be inserted before the beginning of a [beatmap's](Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink") song. Particularly useful if the beatmapper has placed [objects](Glossary#Object "wikilink") right at, or very near to, the beginning of the song.

### Life Bar

Also variously called Health Bar, HP Bar, Spirit Bar or Ki Bar. A bar at the top left of the playing screen which has a symbol moving along it. If the bar empties completely, the player fails the current [beatmap](Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink") (unless he has the Easy, Relax and/or No Fail [mods](mods "wikilink") enabled). The bar [drains](Glossary#Drain "wikilink") at a constant rate throughout the course of a beatmap, except during a [break](break "wikilink"). A sizeable part of the bar is also depleted in case of a [miss](Glossary#Miss "wikilink"). The bar is replenished by the player [Tapping](Glossary#Tap "wikilink") [Hit Circles](Glossary#Hit_Circle "wikilink"), following [Sliders](Slider "wikilink") and Slider Ticks, and spinning [Spinners](Glossary#Spinner "wikilink"). The more accurate the tapping, the bigger the boost. The player also receives larger boosts if he achieves a [Beat!](Score#Katu "wikilink") or an [Elite Beat!](Glossary#Geki "wikilink") at the end of a [combo](combo "wikilink").

M
-

### M4M

Mod For Mod, a term describing when a [Modder](Glossary#Modder "wikilink") agrees to mod another modder's map in return for a mod on his own map

### Mapper

A term given to osu! players that makes [beatmaps](beatmaps "wikilink") from beatmap editor. It can also means the creator of the beatmap in general.

### [MAT](MAT "wikilink")

Short for the [Modding Assistance Team](Modding_Assistance_Team "wikilink"), a group of users experienced in [modding](modding "wikilink"). MAT members can mark [beatmaps](Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink") they approve of with a [bubble](bubble "wikilink"), noting them as worthy of further consideration by the [BAT](BAT "wikilink").

As of the 23rd of April, 2013, all members of the Modding Assistance Team at that time were promoted as full members of the [BAT](BAT "wikilink"), with the MAT being decommissioned.

### Miss

Failing to [tap](Glossary#Tap "wikilink") a [Hit Circle](Glossary#Hit_Circle "wikilink") on time by a large margin, not holding the cursor and/or pressing the button at one or more Slider Ticks, or failing to fill a [Spinner Metre](Glossary#Spinner_Metre "wikilink") within the allotted time. A miss causes a sizeable part of the [Life Bar](Glossary#Life_Bar "wikilink") to be depleted, prevents the player from getting an S, SS, SH or SSH [grade](Glossary#Grade "wikilink") and resets the [Combo](Glossary#Combo "wikilink"), lowering the potential maximum score that can be achieved. Misses in the [DS games](Glossary#DS_games "wikilink") are represented by a red X.

### Modder

A term given to osu! players that gives comments or suggestions on maps of other people at the beatmap editor.

Activities are shown either at [Works In Progress](https://osu.ppy.sh/forum/10) (including the [Modding Queues](https://osu.ppy.sh/forum/60)) and/or [Pending Beatmaps](https://osu.ppy.sh/forum/6).

### NC

i) New Combo, a modding term to suggest that a beat needs to be the start of a new combo.
ii) Nightcore mod, a variant of the double time mod that increases the pitch of the singers and adds a consistent bass drum to the music.

### Nuke

A [beatmap](Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink") which, for various reasons, is currently extremely far from getting [ranked](Ranked_beatmap "wikilink") and needs a particularly large number of modifications before it's even presentable. Examples include not following even the most basic of guidelines, such as having completely incorrect [timing](Glossary#Timing "wikilink") or objects placed at random on the grid and/or timeline, but also [extremely challenging](http://osu.ppy.sh/forum/viewtopic.php?f=6&t=2305&hilit=max+forever) or unconventional beatmaps (e.g. consisting of [a single [Spinner](Glossary#Spinner "wikilink")](http://osu.ppy.sh/forum/viewtopic.php?f=19&t=1498&hilit=leva%27s+polka)). The warning icon that accompanies Nuked beatmaps is the symbol for radioactive materials.

### Object

Collective term for [Hit Circles](Glossary#Hit_Circle "wikilink") [Sliders](Slider "wikilink") and [Spinners](Glossary#Spinner "wikilink"), the gameplay elements players of [osu!](Glossary#osu! "wikilink") and the [DS games](Glossary#DS_games "wikilink") that the player interacts with. Also called "hit objects" or "elements".

### Offset

The period of time between the beginning of a [beatmap’s](Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink") song file and the instant the first beat of the rhythm is heard, from which the position of the first [beat](Glossary#Beat "wikilink") is calculated. Measured in milliseconds (ms). Offset is one half of correct beatmap [timing](Glossary#Timing "wikilink"), the other half being [BPM](BPM "wikilink").

### osu!

i) A rhythm video game for Microsoft Windows, based on certain [DS games](Glossary#DS_games "wikilink"). Spelled with a lowercase "o" and an exclamation mark.
ii) An informal greeting in the Japanese language, resulting from condensing the phrase "**O**hayo gozaima**su**!" (Good morning!) Pronounced with a silent u ("OSS"). Close English equivalents include "Yo!" or "Hey!". Read more about its meaning in Japanese language and culture [here](http://osu.ppy.sh/forum/viewtopic.php?p=2686#p2686).

### Ouendan

i) In Japanese culture, a cheer squad which, similar to western cheerleaders, rallies a sports team on using drums, horns, banners and yells of encouragement. ii) The pair of Japanese [DS games](Glossary#DS_games "wikilink") osu! is based on. Also sometimes used to refer specifically to the first of these games. iii) A collective term for the playable characters in the aforementioned games, i.e. "The Ouendan".

R
-

### Rank

  
*For Ranked beatmaps see [Ranked beatmap](Ranked_beatmap "wikilink").*

i) Another word for a [grade](Glossary#Grade "wikilink").
ii) A player’s standing in the online leaderboard (a.k.a Player [Rankings](Glossary#Ranking "wikilink")).
iii) Verb, "to rank", i.e. to second another moderator’s nomination and include a submitted [beatmap](Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink") to the Ranked Beatmap list, making scores achieved by anyone playing it count towards each player’s Ranking. Getting their beatmaps ranked is a goal of every beatmapper.

### Ranking

Alternatively called Leaderboard. A list which sorts members of the [osu!](Glossary#osu! "wikilink") community according to certain criteria. Can also refer to one's position in such a list. osu! currently has three Rankings: i) Player Rankings: the Ranking most commonly referred to, this list sorts players depending on their Ranked Score. Ranked Score is the sum of the highest score a player has achieved in every [ranked](ranked "wikilink") [beatmap](Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink") he has played. Only [scores](Score "wikilink") achieved after the beatmap is ranked are taken into account. Only one score from each beatmap (the highest) is taken into account, regardless of how many difficulty versions it has. ii) Beatmapper Rankings: This list sorts everyone who has created at least one ranked beatmap, depending on the weighted average of all the ratings given to their beatmaps by other players, multiplied by a bonus modifier that increases the more ranked beatmaps they have. iii) Moderator Rankings: This list sorts moderators depending on how many posts they have made in the Beatmap sub-forums, i.e. how much beatmap [modding](modding "wikilink") they are doing.

### Recalc

Recalculate [Slider](Slider "wikilink") Lengths. A command under the Timing header of the [Editor](Editor "wikilink") screen. Select it when a change in [BPM](BPM "wikilink") is made after Sliders have been placed. This command will try to automatically adjust the length of every Slider in the [beatmap](Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink") to the new BPM. After choosing this command, especially if a large change in BPM has been made, it is important to check every Slider and adjust its length manually where necessary.

### Resnap

A pair of commands under the [Timing](Glossary#Timing "wikilink") header of the [Editor](Editor "wikilink") screen, one of which applies to the entire [beatmap](Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink") and the other to the current [timing section](Glossary#Timing_section "wikilink") (if the beatmap has only one timing section, both commands are the same). Select when there has been a change in [offset](Glossary#Offset "wikilink") and/or [BPM](BPM "wikilink") after [objects](Glossary#Object "wikilink") have been placed. This command will try to automatically reposition all objects on the timeline so that they fall on the closest [beat](Glossary#Beat "wikilink") division your [Snap Divisor](Beat_Snap_Divisor "wikilink") allows. After choosing this command, especially if a large change in BPM and/or offset has been made, it is important to check every object and adjust its position on the timeline manually where necessary.

S
-

### Sample Set

A collection of sound effects that are heard during gameplay. People can use custom sample sets for their [beatmaps](Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink"). Apart from the default sound made when [tapping](Glossary#Tap "wikilink") a [Hit Circle](Glossary#Hit_Circle "wikilink"), the most well known elements in a sample set are the [Whistle](Glossary#Whistle "wikilink") and the [Finish](Glossary#Finish "wikilink"). Other parts of a sample set include the Slider Tick sounds and the sound made when filling the [Spinner Metre](Glossary#Spinner_Metre "wikilink").

### Skin

A set of graphics and custom [sample sets](Glossary#Sample_Set "wikilink") that can be chosen to replace the default ones used by [osu!](Glossary#osu! "wikilink") in order to customise one's playing experience. Most of what you see and hear in osu! can be skinned. There is [a sub-forum dedicated to skins](http://osu.ppy.sh/forum/viewforum.php?f=15).

### Song packs

Collections of [ranked beatmaps](Ranked_beatmap "wikilink") that can be downloaded by file sharing services. Most song packs are sets of beatmaps that were ranked during the same time period, but in other packs contain songs of a similar style or theme. You can find song packs [here](http://osu.ppy.sh/forum/viewtopic.php?f=14&t=1853).

### Spectate

"Spec" for short. To watch another playing a [beatmap](Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink") in real time (approx. 500 ms delay). To spectate, open [Extended Chat](Glossary#Extended_Chat "wikilink") mode and click on a grey user panel. You need to have a copy of the exact same beatmap the person you wish to spectate is playing.

### Spinner

One of three [objects](Glossary#Object "wikilink") the player interacts with (the other two being the [Hit Circle](Glossary#Hit_Circle "wikilink") and the [Slider](Slider "wikilink")) and probably the one seen least often. A Spinner is a circle that takes up the entire height of the play area and has a [Spinner Metre](Glossary#Spinner_Metre "wikilink") on either side. A large circular outline similar to a [Approach Circle](Glossary#Approach_Circle "wikilink") appears on the Spinner’s border and gradually converges on its centre. The player should hold the mouse button down and spin in either a clockwise or anti-clockwise direction. As long as the mouse button is held down, the cursor is locked on the Spinner’s area, so the player need not worry about going outside it. The Spinner is cleared if all the levels of the Spinner Metre are filled before its Approach Circle reaches the centre. Depending on the difficulty settings of each [beatmap](Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink"), a different number of spins are required to fill each level of the Spinner Metre (worth 100 points). If the player fills the Spinner Metre before the Approach Circle reaches the Spinner’s centre, each additional spin before time runs out will award the player with 1000 bonus points (each receiving a bonus multiplier depending on the player’s current [Combo](Combo "wikilink")). Spinning a Spinner provides a continuous minor boost to the [Life Bar](Glossary#Life_Bar "wikilink") depending on the spinning speed.

### Spinner Metre

A pair of bars on either side of a [Spinner](Glossary#Spinner "wikilink") which typically gradually fill up with colour as the player spins the Spinner. In order to clear the spinner, all levels of the Spinner Metre should light up before the [Approach Circle](Glossary#Approach_Circle "wikilink") reaches the Spinner's centre. Each level that lights up on the Spinner Metre awards the player with 100 points (regardless of [Combo](Glossary#Combo "wikilink")).

### Spinner Spam

To overuse [Spinners](Glossary#Spinner "wikilink") in a [beatmap](Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink"). There are many kinds of Spinner Spam: Using very long Spinners (lasting more than 10 seconds or so), using a lot of very short Spinners (lasting less than 2 seconds or so) one after the other, or even worse, using a lot of regular sized or long Spinners one after the other. With the possible exception of some especially challenging beatmaps, Spinner Spam is to be avoided for the following reasons: i) A Spinner is by far the gameplay element that causes the player the most fatigue, since it requires rapid, repetitive, vigorous motion. A lot of Spinners or even a single long one can easily tire a player’s arm. ii) Due to the circular, often frantic motion required to clear it, after clearing a spinner the player’s cursor is often left at a random position in the screen, possibly far from an incoming [Hit Circle](Glossary#Hit_Circle "wikilink") or [Slider](Slider "wikilink"). A multitude of Spinners will thus be particularly disorienting for most players.

### Stacked

[Objects](Glossary#Object "wikilink") that overlap each other in the play field. Most common in [Hit Circles](Glossary#Hit_Circle "wikilink"), less common with Hit Circles and the edges of [sliders](Slider "wikilink") to be avoided with any other combination of objects. Stacking Hit Circles, while a clear violation of [Beat Spacing](Glossary#Beat_Spacing "wikilink"), is nevertheless permitted if the Hit Circles are very close to each other in the timeline (half a [beat](Glossary#Beat "wikilink") or usually even less).

### Standard Submission

A way to submit a [beatmap](Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink") through the [BSS](Glossary#BSS "wikilink") system, making it available to the [osu!](Glossary#osu! "wikilink") community. Standard Submission is the one used when first uploading a beatmap, as well as when updating with changes such as [object](Glossary#Object "wikilink") placement, tweaks in [timing](Glossary#Timing "wikilink"), custom colour schemes, new difficulty versions etc. Every Standard Submission of a beatmap after the first is faster because only the changes are uploaded.

### Star

i) A stage in a beatmap’s [modding](Modding "wikilink") process where a moderator judges a [beatmap](Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink") to be on the right track but needing some improvements before he approves of it with a [bubble](bubble "wikilink"). Examples include adjustments in [timing](Glossary#Timing "wikilink"), incorrect [Beat Spacing](Glossary#Beat_Spacing "wikilink") in some areas, or creating additional difficulty versions. Beatmaps receiving a star are denoted by a star graphic next to their thread’s title. A star may be removed by another mod if he judges the beatmap to need a rather larger amount of improvements than expected.
ii) Can be used as a shortened form of [difficulty](Difficulty "wikilink") stars.

### Star Priority

**Star Priority** （也就是平常说的SP），是帮助 [BN](ZH:BN "wikilink") 寻找有价值[给泡](ZH:Beatmap_Approval#.E6.80.9D.E6.83.B3.E6.B3.A1.E6.B3.A1.EF.BC.88Bubble.EF.BC.89 "wikilink")，进而[给心的谱面](ZH:Beatmap_Approval#.E5.AE.A1.E6.A0.B8.E9.80.9A.E8.BF.87.EF.BC.88Ranked.EF.BC.89 "wikilink")。一张图的 Star Priority 可以间接代表接到了多少个 [Mod](ZH:Modding "wikilink") 并且在这张图给出了多少个 kudosu 星星。每个 [kudosu](ZH:kudosu "wikilink") 星星都可以给**你觉得有价值 rank 的图**（给任何人都行，包括自己，但不要刻意去砸，[这个就是玩坏了的例子](http://osu.ppy.sh/forum/t/87342)）。

如果 star priority 值越高，则谱面在 Works in Progress 版块和 Pending 版块的排名越前（实际上在 Graveyard 版块也是，比如那个 ΔMAX）。通常在 SP 大于等于 12 的时候就可以找BN准备 rank 了。

### Storyboard

A set of still images, animated images and sound effects that tells a story during a [beatmap|beatmap’s](Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink") course but especially before the beatmap’s beginning, during [breaks](Glossary#Break "wikilink") and after the beatmap’s end. In the [DS games](Glossary#DS_games "wikilink"), Storyboards concern one or more persons facing a difficult challenge or predicament which they then try to overcome whilst being cheered on by the player-controlled characters to the tune of a song. Therefore, a Storyboard can take various directions depending on the player’s performance. [osu!](Glossary#osu! "wikilink") storyboarding is supported through the use of a scripting language or the [Storyboard Editor](Storyboard_Editor "wikilink") which can be accessed in [Editor](Editor "wikilink") Mode under "Design". There is [a sub-forum dedicated to Storyboards](http://osu.ppy.sh/forum/viewforum.php?f=20).

### Storyboard Editor

Can be found under "Design" in [Editor](Editor "wikilink") mode. As the name implies, [storyboards](Storyboarding "wikilink") can be easily created and edited here.

### Stream

A long series of [objects](Glossary#Object "wikilink"), not necessarily belonging in a single [Combo](Combo "wikilink"), which appear in rapid succession. Also see [Deathstream](Glossary#Deathstream "wikilink").

### SV

Slider Velocity, the speed in which the slider moves at in relation to the BPM

### TAG2/TAG3/TAG4

Tag difficulties are difficulties that are designed for tag cooperation in multi-play mode. Since each player is responsible for one combo in one turn only, each combo can be separated extremely far, making it nearly impossible to play by a single player (with a mouse, tablet player can play fine), and therefore it becomes a challenge for elite players (with mouse only) in terms of precision of jumping. [http://osu.ppy.sh/b/87570&m=0 Example](http://osu.ppy.sh/b/87570&m=0_Example "wikilink")

TAG difficulties are usually named TAG in a beatmap set. TAG2 maps are meant for 2 players while TAG4 maps (the hardest) are meant for 4 players.

Note that tag difficulties must be in [approved beatmaps](Approved_Beatmap "wikilink") because they are far too hard to be ranked and are not designed for a single player using a mouse only.

### Tablet

A graphics tablet, an alternative input medium to the computer mouse. A tablet consists of a flat surface on which one can point and drag using an attached stylus. The image of the screen does not appear on the tablet, thus making it different in functionality from a [Touch screen](Glossary#Touch_screen "wikilink").

### Tap

To press a button while the cursor is over a [Hit Circle](Glossary#Hit_Circle "wikilink"). Tapping Hit Circles at the right moment, as indicated by the [beatmap’s](Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink") music and the [Approach Circle](Glossary#Approach_Circle "wikilink"), is an essential part of [osu](osu "wikilink")! gameplay. There are many buttons one can use to tap: either mouse button, the z and x keys on the keyboard (see [z/x](Glossary#z/x "wikilink")), either button on a [Touchpad](Glossary#Touchpad "wikilink"), buttons on a [Tablet’s](Glossary#Tablet "wikilink") pen, etc.

### Test Build

A [Build](Glossary#Build "wikilink") usually updated at least daily with up-to-date improvements in the [osu!](Glossary#osu! "wikilink") application. Its [Updater](Glossary#Updater "wikilink") is available to moderators and other willing beta testers upon request. All working changes in the Test Builds are eventually made available in a new version of the [Public Build](Glossary#Public_Build "wikilink").

### Test Mode

A feature at the beatmap editor to test a [Beatmap](Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink") (nothing submitted).

### Time signature

A musical term indicating how many [beats](Glossary#Beat "wikilink") are in each measure and what note value constitutes a single beat. [osu!](Glossary#osu! "wikilink") offers two alternative time signatures, which can be selected from the [Timing](Glossary#Timing "wikilink") menu: 4/4, which is the one most commonly used, and 3/4, which is selected when the rhythm of the song is of, or similar to, a waltz.

### Timing

Probably the most important requirement for a [beatmap](Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink") to get [ranked](ranked "wikilink"), as well as the most difficult to accomplish. Timing a beatmap consists of determining the [offset](Glossary#Offset "wikilink") and then the [BPM](BPM "wikilink") of the song it uses. On songs where the tempo changes, this needs to be done for every [timing section](Glossary#Timing_section "wikilink"). The timing process creates the timeline on which [objects](Glossary#Object "wikilink") will be placed. Without perfect timing, or at least close enough that the human ear can't tell the difference, it is next to impossible to place objects to the rhythm of the music, which is the entire point of [osu!](Glossary#osu! "wikilink")

### Timing section

A part of a song which follows a different rhythm than the parts preceding and following it. Many songs have a constant tempo and thus a single timing section, but for those which have more, the beatmapper needs to identify any areas where the song [BPM](BPM "wikilink") and/ or [time signature](glossary#Time_Signature "wikilink") changes and find the [timing](Glossary#Timing "wikilink") for each one. Timing sections can also be used even if the rhythm of the song remains constant throughout the beatmap, for example in order to allow the beatmapper to assign different [sample sets](Glossary#Sample_Set "wikilink") to different parts of the song. Those are called "inherited timing sections" and are identified by green lines.

### Touch screen

One of the input mediums used by the Nintendo DS and the sole input medium for the [DS games](Glossary#DS_games "wikilink") [osu!](Glossary#osu! "wikilink") is based on. Osu! supports Touch screen functionality, but the hardware required is generally expensive for the average player. A computer whose entire screen functions as a Touch screen is called a Tablet PC (Do not confuse with [Tablet](Glossary#Tablet "wikilink")).

### Touchpad

A substitute for the computer mouse found on laptop computers. A small rectangular area on which one can drag one’s finger to move the mouse cursor. Due to the way touchpads are designed, they do not work with a stylus or similar devices.

### Universal Offset

The time delay between a) the [object](Glossary#Object "wikilink") graphics and [sample set](Glossary#Sample_Set "wikilink") sounds associated with them and b) the point of the song where they should be heard. Universal Offset applies to all songs used in [osu!](Glossary#osu! "wikilink") and its value differs according to each system's specifications. After being determined, it doesn't need to be modified again. Incorrectly set Universal Offset causes [timing](Glossary#Timing "wikilink") problems in every [beatmap](Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink") played or edited. If everything sounds off to you while playing a [ranked](Ranked_beatmap "wikilink") [beatmap](Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink"), try checking your Universal Offset. Universal Offset is set from the Options Menu.

### Updater

The osume.exe file, which, when run, will check whether there have been updates to the [osu!](Glossary#osu! "wikilink") [build](Glossary#Build "wikilink") you are using and download and install any such updates. There are two Updaters - one for the [Public Build](Glossary#Public_Build "wikilink") and one for the [Test Build](Glossary#Test_Build "wikilink").

"wikilink") community, forget about depositing it on Rapidshare, Megaupload, ImageShack and the like - put it on upppy and everyone's happy! :) You can find it [here](http://up.ppy.sh).

### Whistle

A sound effect, part of a [sample set](Glossary#Sample_Set "wikilink"), usually reserved for emphasizing and punctuating certain points in a song. Its default form and icon in the [Editor](Editor "wikilink") is that of a blown whistle.

### WIP

Acronym for Work In Progress, a [beatmap](Glossary#Beatmap "wikilink") that is, for one reason or another, deemed incomplete by its creator (e.g. incomplete difficulty versions, only one difficulty version, pending correction or suggestions on the timing etc.) WIPs can be submitted through the [BSS](Glossary#BSS "wikilink") to the Beatmap Help forum to get comments, feedback and help from [mods](Modding "wikilink") and other users.

### z/x

To use the z and x keyboard keys to [tap](Glossary#Tap "wikilink") in [osu!](Glossary#osu! "wikilink"). A fairly popular alternative to the left and right mouse buttons. During gameplay, while z is pressed, the left mouse button will not work and vice versa. The same applies to the x key and the right mouse button. This can be changed in the options menu.
